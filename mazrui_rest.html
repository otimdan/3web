Our Story

Rooted in Uganda. Inspired by Africa.

Mazuri Kitchen was born in 2017 with a single conviction: that East African food deserves its seat at the world's finest tables. We work directly with smallholder farmers in Wakiso, Mpigi, and Mbarara — buying fresh at source, arriving daily, cooked with care.

From smoky matoke served in banana leaf to slow-braised goat with herb-infused groundnut sauce, every dish on our menu carries the fingerprint of someone who grew it, harvested it, and trusted us with it.

Our Kitchen Philosophy

The Open Kitchen Approach

Our kitchen has no hidden doors. Guests are welcome to watch, smell, and ask questions — because transparency is how we build trust, one dish at a time.

  • Every ingredient has a named source — we publish our farm partners quarterly.
  • Our head chef trained at the East African Culinary Institute and spent two years cooking across Rwanda, Tanzania, and Ethiopia.
  • We bake our bread in-house daily using heritage grain from Kabale.
  • No MSG, no artificial flavour boosters — just technique, time, and good ingredients.
